Exercise: Learn About Search

In this walkthrough exercise, you learn about search by viewing the Concepts Guide, and then exploring a search application.

 

  1. In a browser window, navigate to docs.marklogic.com/guide/concepts/search.
  2. See that you are viewing the Search chapter in the Concepts Guide:

  3. Select a topic of interest (e.g. Search APIs).
  4. Skim through the topic.
  5. Continue perusing as desired.
  6. If desired, access the Search Developer's Guide by navigating to docs.marklogic.com/guide/search-dev.
  7. Skim the table of contents, to get an idea of the search capabilities in MarkLogic:


  8. In a browser, navigate to mlu.marklogic.com/top-songs.
  9. Notice the faceted navigation, the search field, the advanced search link, and the birthday search field:


  10. In the results area, notice the number of results, the paging links, the sort options, and the more links.
  11. Notice that the default results are the 10 most recent songs in the database.
  12. In the search field, enter seeing.
  13. See that, with MarkLogic's ability to do stemming, results are returned for see, seen, and seeing.
  14. Realize that the facets are also narrowed down.
  15. Click on the page banner, to display the facet values based on all of the documents in the database.
  16. In the search field, enter love.
  17. See that the facet values are narrowed down to only documents that contain the word love.
  18. In the decade list, select 1960s, to further narrow down the list to songs that were a number 1 hit in the 1960s.
  19. In the artist list, select the beatles to additionally narrow down the results to songs where the artist is The Beatles.
  20. See that there are recommendations:


  21. Realize that these recommendations are made possible by triples in the database (e.g. The Beatles - band member - John Lennon).
  22. If desired, in the birthday search field, enter your birthday to see what song was #1 in the U.S. the week you were born.
  23. Click the advanced search link.
  24. See that you could do a very specific search.
  25. Continue perusing, as desired.
